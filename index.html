Princess Diana

Queen of Hearts


  1. 1961: Born in Sandringham Norfolk (England)

  2. 1970: At the age of 9, Diana enrolls at Riddlesworth Hall, an all-girls boarding school in Norfolk.

  3. 1975: Diana inherits the title of Lady after her grandfather’s death.

  4. 1977: Diana first meets her future husband, Prince Charles

  5. 1981: Charles and Diana publicly announce their engagement, with Diana debuting her famous sapphire-and-diamond ring. The couple got married in the same year.

  6. 1982: On June 21, Diana gives birth to her first son, Prince William Arthur Philip Louis.

  7. 1984: Diana welcomes her second son, Prince Henry Charles Albert David. At this stage, she continues to prioritize her family while also engaging in her royal duties.

  8. 1985: Diana’s humanitarian efforts begin to take center stage. She visits AIDS patients at the Middlesex Hospital in London, challenging social stigmas and advocating for compassion and understanding.

  9. 1987: Diana’s involvement with AIDS awareness intensifies. She famously shakes hands with an AIDS patient without gloves, dispelling misconceptions about transmission. Her actions have a profound impact on public perception.

  10. 1989: Diana visits Harlem Hospital in New York City, where she meets children suffering from HIV/AIDS. Her compassion and warmth resonate deeply with those she interacts with

  11. 1991: The International Red Cross appoints Diana as its honorary president. She uses her position to raise awareness about the global landmine crisis. Her advocacy leads to the Ottawa Treaty, which aims to ban anti-personnel landmines.

  12. 1992: Diana’s marriage to Charles faces challenges, and they officially separate. Despite personal struggles, she remains committed to her charitable work

  13. 1995: Diana visits Bosnia to meet with landmine victims. Her empathy and determination contribute to the ongoing campaign for landmine removal and rehabilitation

  14. 1997: On August 31, Diana tragically dies in a car crash in Paris. Her death shocks the world, but her legacy lives on. Her humanitarian efforts continue to inspire generations.
